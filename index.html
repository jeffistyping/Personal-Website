Pressure Lock

A pressure activated system that allows an individual to unlock/lock their door by knocking on the pressure pad in a specific pattern. It works by implementing an initial preset pattern that can be changed by the user and multiple users are able to set up their own patterns. When the pressure sensor detects pressure, it will check if the input matches the saved pattern. If the inputted pattern matches, the motor will be activated and unlock the door.

5km to 42.2Km

I was never good at running and the summer of 2016 I wanted to challenge myself with the goal of completing a full marathon within a year. I knew it was an ambitious goal and I wasn't going to wake up tomorrow and run 42.2Km. At first, I committed myself to running twice a week after school and when I felt like I was ready, I ran my first 5km race. At that point onwards the races went from 5km to 10km, 12km, 15km 21.1km, 30km, 35km. At one point I was training 5 times a week with a race on the weekend for 3 months straight. By May 6, 2017, I complete the Vancouver Marathon. I explored many different types of races, I did road running, trail running and obstacle courses. My favorite by far is trail running and this lead me into becoming an avid trail runner/hiker. This challenge is significant to me because not only did I complete a goal which I thought would have been impossible a year ago, but also for each race I ran, it donated to a cause that I support.
I ran a total of 30 races at the end of this.
